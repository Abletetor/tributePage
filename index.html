Dr Kwame Nkrumah

The man who lead Ghana to independence

A Political Activism

Here's a time line of Dr. Kwame Nkrumah's life:

  • - Born on September 21, 1909, in Nkroful, Gold Coast (now Ghana)
  • - Educated in the United States and England, where he became influenced by Pan-Africanism and African nationalism.
  • - Founded the Convention People's Party in 1949 and led the country to independence from British colonial rule in 1957.
  • - Served as the first Prime Minister and President of Ghana.
  • - Advocated for socialism and implemented policies aimed at modernizing Ghana's economy.
  • - Emphasized the importance of African unity and was a leading figure in the Pan-African movement.
  • - Overthrown in a coup in 1966 and lived in exile until his death on April 27, 1972, in Bucharest, Romania.
  • - Remembered as a visionary leader who played a crucial role in Ghana's independence and the broader African liberation movement.

"Dr. Nkrumah Kwame was an inspirational leader whose dedication to the independence and development of Ghana has left a lasting impact on the world. His vision for a united and prosperous Africa continues to inspire future generations."

-- Nelson Mandela
